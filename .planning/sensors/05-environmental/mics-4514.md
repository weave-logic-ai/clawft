## Overview

The SGX MiCS-4514 is a dual metal-oxide sensor on one die: the **RED** element targets reducing gases (CO, H₂, ethanol) and the **OX** element targets oxidizing gases (NO₂, ozone). It's the go-to cheap sensor for **traffic pollution** (NO₂ is a vehicle exhaust marker) when a real electrochemical probe is out of budget.

## Key specs

- RED (reducing): 1–1000 ppm CO, 1–500 ppm H₂.
- OX (oxidizing): 0.05–10 ppm NO₂.
- Two independent heaters, each with its own power rail and warm-up curve.
- Output: analog (RED and OX resistances via load resistors).
- Warm-up: several minutes cold-start; 24+ h first-ever burn-in.
- Life: ~5 years typical.

## Interface & wiring

- Heater pins: `RED_H` (~2.4 V, ~43 mA) and `OX_H` (~1.7 V, ~26 mA). Most breakouts include the correct voltage regulation; some require external regulation.
- Sense: `RED_S` and `OX_S` go to separate voltage dividers → two ADC channels. Use an [ADS1115](../10-storage-timing-power/ads1115-ads1015.md) for clean reads.
- 5 V supply; logic is analog so 3.3 V MCUs read through the ADC fine.
- Mount somewhere with airflow — stagnant enclosures bias readings.

## Benefits

- Two gases per chip, very compact.
- NO₂ sensitivity is the killer feature — no other hobby-tier sensor catches vehicle exhaust.
- Low power compared to two separate MQ-series sensors.
- Smaller footprint than MQ family.

## Limitations / gotchas

- Same metal-oxide caveats as [MQ series](./mq-series.md): **cross-sensitivity, humidity / temperature dependence, multi-day burn-in**. Treat as trend indicators.
- Two heaters with different voltages — wiring the wrong one at 5 V destroys the element.
- Some "MiCS-4514" AliExpress boards are actually MiCS-2714 (one gas only).
- Calibration drifts; for regulatory air-quality work, use a real electrochemical NO₂ cell.
- Long lifetime but needs periodic re-zero in known clean air.

## Typical use cases

- Near-road air quality / traffic studies.
- Indoor cooking (gas stove NO₂) monitoring.
- Combustion exhaust detection (furnace, gas water heater).
- Paired with PM and CO₂ for a full outdoor-air-quality node.

## Where to buy

- DFRobot Gravity I²C breakout (nice all-in-one with built-in ADC).

## Software / libraries

- DFRobot publishes a C++ library for their I²C version.
- For bare sensors: no driver — read two ADC channels, apply per-element curves from the datasheet.

## Notes for WeftOS

- Tag MiCS readings as "relative, uncalibrated NO₂" — never publish ppb without regulatory-grade calibration.
- HAL enforces burn-in counter (NVS-backed) before the sensor is considered "ready".
- Pair with the traffic-pollution dashboard in WeftOS Admin: NO₂ + PM2.5 + wind direction makes a genuinely useful "is the road polluting us right now" view.
