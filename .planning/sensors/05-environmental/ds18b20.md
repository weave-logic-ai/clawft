## Overview

The DS18B20 is the classic Maxim/ADI 1-Wire digital temperature sensor — 9 to 12-bit resolution, −55 to +125 °C, and available as a pre-wired waterproof stainless probe. Multi-drop on a single wire means dozens of sensors can share one GPIO. For WeftOS, it's the default for water tanks, reservoirs, soil, compost piles, and HVAC duct temperatures.

## Key specs

- Range: −55 to +125 °C; ± 0.5 °C (−10 to +85 °C).
- Resolution: 9/10/11/12 bit (selectable; 12-bit = 0.0625 °C).
- Conversion time: 750 ms at 12-bit; faster at lower resolution.
- Interface: Maxim 1-Wire. Parasitic power mode uses 2 wires (data + GND).
- Each sensor has a unique 64-bit ROM ID — multi-drop is identity-based.

## Interface & wiring

- 3-wire (recommended): `VCC`, `GND`, `DATA` + 4.7 kΩ pull-up on `DATA` to `VCC`.
- 2-wire parasitic: `GND` + `DATA` (powers itself off the bus cap). Works but wrecks long-cable reliability.
- Multi-drop: multiple probes on the same `DATA` pin; each is addressed by ROM ID.
- Long cable runs (> 5 m) can be unreliable at high bus speed; use shielded twisted pair and lower the pull-up to 2.2 kΩ.
- ESP32 1-Wire driver needs tight timing — use the RMT-based library for reliability.

## Benefits

- Probe form factor is ideal for liquids (stainless tube, epoxy-sealed).
- Multi-drop: one GPIO for many probes.
- Well-calibrated from factory; no per-unit tuning needed.
- Cheap, rugged, waterproof.

## Limitations / gotchas

- 750 ms conversion at 12-bit is *slow* — plan scheduling.
- Parasitic power mode is flaky on long cables or many drops — use 3-wire for production.
- Counterfeit DS18B20s are common on AliExpress; they work in typical conditions but drift at extremes. The ROM ID family code is always 0x28 — clones respect that but lie about calibration.
- 1-Wire is timing-sensitive; some WiFi-heavy ESP32 sketches miss bits. Use RMT / dedicated driver.
- If the probe tip's epoxy is cracked, water wicks up the cable and shorts — inspect on install.

## Typical use cases

- Water tank temperature (aquarium, reservoir, hot water heater).
- Soil temperature at multiple depths (one GPIO, N probes).
- Compost pile monitoring.
- HVAC duct inlet/outlet deltas.

## Notes for WeftOS

- The HAL should enumerate 1-Wire devices and expose each by ROM ID + a human-friendly label from config.
- Schedule conversions asynchronously — kick off, do other work, read back after 750 ms.
- Warn the user when a probe's response starts differing from its historical neighbour by > 2 °C (likely water ingress).
