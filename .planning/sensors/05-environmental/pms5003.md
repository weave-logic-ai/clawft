## Overview

The Plantower PMS5003 is a laser-diode particle counter: a small fan pulls air past a 650 nm laser, and a photodetector counts scattering events in size bins to report PM1.0, PM2.5, and PM10 mass concentrations (µg/m³) and particle counts per 0.1 L. For WeftOS it's *the* canonical particulate sensor — what you want for wildfire smoke, cooking PM, and dust alerts.

## Key specs

- Measures PM1.0, PM2.5, PM10 in µg/m³ plus particle-count bins (>0.3, >0.5, >1.0, >2.5, >5.0, >10 µm).
- Effective range: 0–500 µg/m³ (linear).
- Response time: ~10 s.
- Interface: UART (9600 8N1) framed with a CRC; PMS5003 also has an I²C variant (PMSA003).
- Laser lifetime: ~8000 hours of continuous operation → ~1 year always-on, ~3+ years duty-cycled.
- Fan pulls ~100 mA at 5 V.

## Interface & wiring

- UART: 5 V supply; TX/RX are 3.3 V logic (friendly to ESP32 without shifters).
- SET pin: pull high for active mode, low for standby (fan off, low power).
- RESET pin.
- Mount with the intake facing the ambient air and exhaust unobstructed; back-pressure corrupts counts.
- Don't run in active mode continuously unless you need sub-minute latency — duty-cycle 30 s on / 2–5 min off to extend laser life.

## Benefits

- Real laser-based particle measurement in the same class as reference-grade sensors (at 1/50 the price).
- Directly interpretable in µg/m³ (no fudging).
- Fast response — catches cooking / smoking events within seconds of the fan starting.
- Well-supported by HomeAssistant, ESPHome, and the research community.

## Limitations / gotchas

- **Laser lifetime.** Continuous operation wears out the laser in ~1 year. Duty-cycle aggressively — the HAL should default to on-demand sampling (e.g., 30 s/5 min) and only switch to continuous during active events.
- Fan noise is audible in quiet rooms — not for bedrooms without a sleep mode.
- Humidity sensitivity: high RH (> 70 %) inflates PM2.5 readings because water droplets look like particles. Apply a humidity-compensation curve (available in Plantower app notes).
- Calibration shift at extreme low counts (< 5 µg/m³) is noisy; 0 µg/m³ is sometimes reported in truly clean air.
- Checksum failures under bus noise; the library must validate and retry.
- Fake / clone sensors sold on AliExpress with no laser — they'll report constant 0 µg/m³.

## Typical use cases

- Wildfire-smoke alerts.
- Cooking / smoking PM events (fans on, windows open).
- Air purifier on/off automation.
- Classroom / office air-quality dashboards.
- Full air-quality node (pair with MH-Z19 CO₂ and BME680 VOC).

## Notes for WeftOS

- Default HAL policy: duty-cycle 30 s active / 5 min sleep. Apps can request "continuous" for at most N minutes at a time, with a dose budget against the 8000 h laser lifetime.
- Apply humidity compensation when a companion [BME280](./bme280.md) is present.
- Surface PM2.5 as a primary dashboard metric alongside CO₂ — these two together cover most "should I open a window?" decisions.
- Log raw particle-count bins in addition to µg/m³ — researchers love them, and they survive firmware updates to the mass-conversion formula.
