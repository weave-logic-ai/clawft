## Overview

The BME680 (and newer BME688) adds a heated metal-oxide VOC gas-resistance channel to the [BME280](./bme280.md)'s T/RH/P measurements. Raw gas resistance is a relative signal — when VOCs rise, resistance drops — but the **indoor air quality (IAQ) index** that people expect is computed by Bosch's **BSEC** (Bosch Sensortec Environmental Cluster) closed-source library. BME688 adds on-device ML for custom VOC pattern recognition ("smells like burnt toast").

## Key specs

- Temperature / humidity / pressure: same as BME280.
- Gas sensor: metal oxide with heater, cycled ~1 per 3 s.
- IAQ (via BSEC): 0–500 index, ± 15 % accuracy claimed.
- Heater temperature: up to 400 °C (in short pulses).
- Interfaces: I²C (0x76/0x77) or SPI.
- BME688 adds built-in AI core (BHI ML) for up to 4 VOC classes.

## Interface & wiring

- Identical to BME280 for I²C / SPI pins.
- BSEC library on ESP32: needs ~14 KB RAM and ~150 KB flash; must run a specific measurement cadence (3 s ultra-low-power or 300 s LP) or IAQ drifts.
- Heater pulses cause short current spikes (~12 mA); keep the 3.3 V rail within spec.

## Benefits

- T/RH/P + gas in one package; great for all-in-one IAQ nodes.
- BSEC gives a Bosch-calibrated IAQ index with minimal host work.
- BME688 enables custom smell / gas classification with on-chip ML.
- Long lifetime vs older MQ-series metal-oxide sensors.

## Limitations / gotchas

- **BSEC is a precompiled binary blob with a non-OSS license.** Bosch permits "use with a BME680/BME688", but redistributing the blob has restrictions — check the current BSEC license before shipping WeftOS images that include it. Raw gas resistance is fine to use without BSEC.
- First power-up requires ~5 day burn-in for stable IAQ (~30 min for rough readings). Persist calibration state in NVS and feed it back on every boot.
- Gas resistance is a relative signal — can't be compared between two BME680s without co-calibration.
- Heater will create a ~0.5 °C local bias on the T channel; factor out or mount sensor isolated.
- Cheap AliExpress BME680 boards are often BME280 chips in disguise; verify chip ID 0x61 (BME680) or 0x61 (BME688 — read extra regs).
- BME688 ML ("BSEC2") has a different API and toolchain than BSEC1.

## Typical use cases

- Indoor air quality (IAQ) dashboard.
- "Did the dinner burn?" / cooking detection with BME688 ML.
- Baseline for a full air-quality node (pair with MH-Z19 CO₂ and PMS5003 PM).
- VOC trend logging in labs.

## Software / libraries

- `bme680` Rust crate for raw gas resistance + T/RH/P (no BSEC IAQ).
- Bosch's BSEC1 / BSEC2 distribution (requires registration on bosch-sensortec.com) — precompiled for ESP32, Linux, Cortex-M.
- `BSEC Arduino Library` for hobby IAQ.

## Notes for WeftOS

- Offer two modes in the HAL: "raw gas resistance" (fully open) vs "IAQ via BSEC" (requires licence acknowledgement in the build).
- Persist BSEC state blob across reboots — without it, IAQ recalibrates every boot (user-visible drift).
- For BME688, expose the ML model slot as a pluggable asset WeftOS apps can ship signed models into.
- Because it's a product-tier VOC sensor, BME680/688 readings are suitable for alerting — but treat IAQ as a *trend indicator*, not absolute air quality.
