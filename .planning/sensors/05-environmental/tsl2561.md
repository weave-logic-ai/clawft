## Overview

The TSL2561 is a two-channel light sensor: one photodiode covers visible + IR, the other only IR. By subtracting, it computes a lux value that approximates human perception. Older than [BH1750](./bh1750-lux.md) but offers **separate IR and visible channels**, which is occasionally useful for detecting [IR illuminators](../04-light-display/ir-flood-arrays.md) or discriminating sunlight from incandescent. The newer TSL2591 extends the range and is a drop-in successor.

## Key specs

- Range: ~0.1 to 40000 lux (TSL2561); up to 88000 lux (TSL2591).
- Two channels: broadband (visible + IR) and IR-only.
- Interface: I²C, addresses 0x29 / 0x39 / 0x49.
- Programmable gain (1x / 16x) and integration time (13 ms / 101 ms / 402 ms).
- Low power: sub-mA active.

## Interface & wiring

- I²C: standard `SDA/SCL/VCC/GND`; `ADDR` or `ADDR_SEL` pin selects one of three I²C addresses.
- Interrupt pin available for threshold-driven wake.
- 3.3 V only.

## Benefits

- Wide dynamic range (sunlight to near-dark) thanks to programmable gain + integration.
- Separate IR channel lets you detect IR-only illumination (e.g., your own [IR flood array](../04-light-display/ir-flood-arrays.md)) vs ambient.
- Three I²C addresses → up to three sensors on one bus without a mux.

## Limitations / gotchas

- Lux formula is a piecewise fit — edge cases (heavy IR from incandescent, LED lighting) give 10–30 % error vs true photopic lux.
- At max gain + long integration, readings saturate fast in direct sun; need to shorten integration dynamically.
- TSL2561 is mostly superseded by TSL2591 (same vendor, same library family) and APDS-9960; stock availability has dropped.
- Address selection via analog voltage on `ADDR_SEL` on some parts — easy to wire wrong.

## Typical use cases

- Sensor array where you want to detect the presence of your own [IR flood](../04-light-display/ir-flood-arrays.md) alongside ambient visible light.
- Wider-range daylight logging than BH1750.
- Multi-sensor arrays (3 per bus) for directional light mapping.

## Notes for WeftOS

- For new deployments prefer BH1750 (cheaper, simpler) or APDS-9960 (more features) unless the IR channel is specifically needed.
- The IR channel is useful for WeftOS privacy checks: a camera node can verify its own IR illuminator is on (without looking at the image) before enabling night-vision capture.
