## Overview

The APDS-9960 is a combined ambient-light + RGB color + proximity + gesture sensor in a single I²C chip. Famously the sensor behind early Samsung Galaxy "wave-to-unlock" gestures. For WeftOS it's the "Swiss Army" optical sensor: point a node at a hand and get swipes; point it at a surface and get RGB reflectance; use the proximity channel to wake a display.

## Key specs

- Ambient light: 0.01 to 64000 lux (human-eye approx).
- RGB color: 16-bit R/G/B channels.
- Proximity: 8-bit, 0–100 mm typical.
- Gesture: 4-direction (up, down, left, right) at ~100–200 Hz internally, up to ~20 cm range.
- Interface: I²C, fixed address 0x39.
- Built-in IR LED for proximity / gesture.

## Interface & wiring

- I²C: `SDA`, `SCL`, interrupt pin `INT` (open-drain).
- 3.3 V; board usually has on-board LDO.
- IR LED drive is internal; don't obstruct the sensor window with opaque enclosure.
- Gesture mode uses a FIFO; poll on `INT` falling edge rather than constant I²C polling.

## Benefits

- Four distinct sensing modalities on one $5–12 chip.
- Gesture detection works at 10–20 cm without training.
- Color sensing is usable for surface detection (white paper vs green leaf, etc.).
- Low power — proximity at < 1 mA average.

## Limitations / gotchas

- **Address is fixed at 0x39** — multiple sensors on one bus need a mux.
- Gesture range drops below 5 cm → becomes "proximity only".
- Cheap clones sometimes omit the IR LED correctly (marked I2C-only) — no gesture/proximity without the LED.
- RGB counts are not absolute color — no built-in white-balance; needs per-sensor calibration for color classification.
- Sunlight saturates the gesture channel outdoors; indoor-only reliably.
- Glass or clear plastic over the sensor must be IR-transmissive.

## Typical use cases

- Wake-on-wave for displays (hover hand to light up).
- Gesture navigation on a WeftOS appliance without buttons.
- Surface color classification (e.g., sorting coloured objects).
- Short-range presence detection (is a hand hovering over the kiosk?).

## Notes for WeftOS

- Treat as a multi-modal sensor: emit `AmbientLux`, `ProximityEvent`, `GestureEvent`, `ColorSample` on separate channels so apps subscribe only to what they need.
- Integrate gestures into the same event bus as touch / keyboard — apps should be able to accept "swipe left" the same way they accept "left arrow".
- Turn off gesture mode when the proximity channel is idle for > 30 s to save power and LED life.
