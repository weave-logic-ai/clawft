## Overview

CCS811 (AMS, now discontinued but widely stocked) and ENS160 (ScioSense, the spiritual successor) are I²C metal-oxide gas sensors that output a total VOC (TVOC) concentration and an **equivalent CO₂** (eCO₂) number. eCO₂ is **derived** from VOC — not a true CO₂ measurement — which is a perennial source of confusion. For WeftOS, they are a cheaper alternative to running a [BME680 with BSEC](./bme680-bme688.md) when you only care about VOC trends.

## Key specs

| | CCS811 | ENS160 |
|-|--------|--------|
| TVOC | 0–1187 ppb | 0–65000 ppb |
| eCO₂ | 400–8192 ppm | 400–65000 ppm |
| Heater | yes (built-in) | yes |
| Warm-up | 20 min cold, 48 h first-ever | ~3 min cold |
| Address | 0x5A / 0x5B | 0x52 / 0x53 |
| Status | Discontinued (NRND) | Active |

## Interface & wiring

- I²C with `WAK` / `WAKE` pin that must be tied low when talking to the chip (CCS811).
- ENS160 supports a simpler "always on" mode.
- Pair with a T/RH sensor (BME280, AHT20) and **write compensated values** back to the gas sensor — readings drift significantly with humidity and temperature.
- 3.3 V logic; 5 V will damage the die.

## Benefits

- Single chip, I²C, gives "TVOC" and "eCO₂" numbers out of the box.
- Much lower power than MQ-series (no big external heater at 150 mA).
- Works with the popular Adafruit / DFRobot / SparkFun breakouts.

## Limitations / gotchas

- **eCO₂ is not real CO₂.** It's a back-calculation from VOC assuming human-exhaled breath. In a kitchen (alcohol vapors), it will massively over-report "CO₂". Never use for ventilation control without a real NDIR sensor like [MH-Z19](./mh-z19-co2.md).
- **CCS811 is NRND.** New designs should use ENS160.
- **Long initial burn-in.** CCS811 spec is 48 hours first-time. ENS160 is much better but still benefits from a 24 h burn-in.
- VOC baseline must be saved periodically to non-volatile storage and restored on reboot, or the sensor re-learns its baseline each boot and first readings are wrong.
- Humidity / temperature compensation must be pushed to the device; without it, readings shift.
- Clones occasionally ship with dud metal-oxide elements; an ENS160 board that reports constant 400 ppm eCO₂ is a common dead-element failure mode.

## Typical use cases

- Indoor air "smell" tracking (cooking, cleaning, off-gassing).
- Occupancy inference (humans exhale VOCs + CO₂ together — eCO₂ rises with people).
- Low-cost IAQ dashboards where a real CO₂ sensor is out of budget.

## Notes for WeftOS

- Always render the reading as "TVOC + eCO₂ (derived)" in UI; never drop the "derived" tag.
- HAL persists baseline blob in NVS every 6–24 h (vendor-recommended cadence).
- When paired with [MH-Z19](./mh-z19-co2.md), WeftOS should annotate divergences ("eCO₂ high but CO₂ flat → probably VOCs, not people").
