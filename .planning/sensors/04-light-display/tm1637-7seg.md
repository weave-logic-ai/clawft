## Overview

The TM1637 is a cheap LED display driver that most often ships on 4-digit 7-segment breakout boards, typically with a colon between digits 2 and 3 (great for clocks). The chip also supports an 8×2 keypad matrix on spare pins, though few breakouts expose it. For WeftOS, it's the right part when "I just want to show a number" and a 128×64 OLED is overkill.

## Key specs

- 4 digits × 7 segments + colon + decimal point (board-dependent).
- 8 brightness levels.
- Proprietary 2-wire serial (CLK + DIO) — **not** I²C, even though it uses similar-looking SDA/SCL-style pins.
- Optional 8×2 keypad scan (rare on hobbyist boards).
- Voltage: 3.3 V or 5 V; logic is tolerant of 3.3 V from ESP32 GPIOs.

## Interface & wiring

- Four pins: `VCC`, `GND`, `CLK`, `DIO`.
- Protocol: start / stop conditions plus 8-bit data, ACK per byte. Not I²C-compatible — don't put it on the I²C bus.
- Bit-bang from any two GPIOs; no hardware peripheral needed.
- Keep CLK/DIO wires short; no bus pull-ups required in practice.

## Benefits

- Dollar-level cost, trivial to drive, looks like a real clock / counter.
- Readable from across a room with zero UI framework overhead.
- Almost no RAM or flash cost — fine on the smallest MCUs.

## Limitations / gotchas

- 4 digits only (unless you chain multiple modules, which vendor libs don't always support).
- **Not I²C**, despite pin labels suggesting otherwise — don't share the bus.
- Fixed 7-segment font — no icons beyond colon/dot.
- Brightness steps are coarse; at low ambient light even step 0 is quite bright.
- Some clones have the colon hard-wired on/off; verify before buying if you need a blinking colon.

## Typical use cases

- Wall clock on a voice terminal.
- Running counter (unread messages, people counted, items dispensed).
- Temperature / humidity readout for a quick glance.
- "Score" / timer display for physical games or reaction toys.

- `TM1637` (Arduino) for the quickest path.

## Notes for WeftOS

- Expose a `NumericDisplay4` HAL interface: `set_number`, `set_colon`, `set_brightness`. Apps should never touch segment bytes directly.
- Brightness policy tied to [`../05-environmental/bh1750-lux.md`](../05-environmental/bh1750-lux.md) — dim automatically at night.
- Clock colons blinking is a "heartbeat" proxy — if the colon stops, the node is wedged.
