## Overview

The TB6612FNG (Toshiba) and L9110S are MOSFET-based dual H-bridges — the
modern successors to the L298N. They drop far less voltage (~0.5 V total per
bridge vs 2 V), accept **3.3 V logic directly**, and fit on a stamp-sized
breakout. TB6612FNG is the stronger of the two (1.2 A continuous, 3 A peak);
L9110S / HG7881 is even cheaper and smaller at around 800 mA.

For anything battery-powered, or anything smaller than a full-size gearmotor,
these are the right default.

## Key specs

| Spec | TB6612FNG | L9110S |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Bridges | 2 | 2 |
| Continuous current | ~1.2 A | ~800 mA |
| Peak current | 3 A (10 s) | ~1.5 A |
| Motor rail | 2.5–13.5 V | 2.5–12 V |
| Logic | 2.7–5.5 V, 3.3 V-compatible | 2.5–12 V |
| Bridge drop | ~0.5 V total | ~1 V |
| Standby | Yes (via `STBY` pin, TB6612) | No |

## Interface & wiring

TB6612: per channel `AIN1` / `AIN2` set direction, `PWMA` takes PWM, `STBY`
(tied high) enables the chip. L9110S: two `IN` pins per channel; you can
drive one as PWM and the other as direction, or use both for bi-directional
PWM. Supply motor rail on `VM`, logic rail on `VCC`. Common the grounds.

## Benefits

- 3.3 V-compatible logic inputs — no level shifter.
- Low bridge drop → better battery life, less heat.
- TB6612's `STBY` pin lets you park the chip in microamps during MCU sleep.
- Tiny footprint — both fit on breadboard.

## Limitations / gotchas

- Not for giant motors. Above ~1 A continuous, move to a DRV8871 / DRV8833 /
  BTS7960.
- L9110S has no standby and runs slightly warmer; fine for small gearmotors,
  less so for robots that park often.
- Exposed thermal pad on TB6612FNG modules — keep a bit of airflow on the
  tiny breakout PCB.

## Typical use cases

- Small-robot drivetrains (two gearmotors).
- Gimbal / cart actuators.
- Paired solenoid / pump switching when you also want reverse.

## Software / libraries

Plain PWM + two GPIOs per channel. SparkFun publishes an Arduino `TB6612FNG`
library that wraps direction / speed helpers; not strictly needed.

## Notes for WeftOS

Default motor-driver for new WeftOS designs. Mark `STBY` as a first-class
control so the scheduler can deep-sleep the bridge between moves.
