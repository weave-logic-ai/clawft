## Overview

The INA219 and INA260 are TI **high-side** current monitors: they sit in series
with the positive rail, measure the voltage drop across a shunt resistor, and
report bus voltage, current, and power over I²C. INA219 uses an **external**
shunt (you size it to your current range); INA260 **integrates a 2 mΩ shunt**
and removes a whole source of user error at the cost of flexibility. Both
integrate a 12-bit ADC and a multiplier so the `power` register is direct.

## Key specs

| Spec | INA219 | INA260 |
|------|--------|--------|
| Bus voltage | 0 – 26 V | 0 – 36 V |
| Current range | ±3.2 A with default 0.1 Ω shunt | ±15 A (integrated 2 mΩ) |
| Resolution | ~0.1 mA typical | 1.25 mA |
| Voltage accuracy | ±0.5 % | ±0.2 % |
| I²C address | 0x40 – 0x4F (4 variants) | 0x40 – 0x4F |

## Interface & wiring

- I²C, default address 0x40. Both parts have 4-bit address configuration so
  you can stack up to 16 on one bus.
- **High-side** topology: shunt is in the + rail between supply and load. Wire
  `V+` → load side of shunt, `VIN+`/`VIN–` across the shunt. INA260 has just
  two power terminals and is almost impossible to wire wrong.
- INA219: pick a shunt value so full-scale current gives < 320 mV across it
  (chip limit); 0.1 Ω is default for ±3.2 A, 0.01 Ω for ±32 A.
- Hot tip: with Wi-Fi running, ESP32 can pull 500 mA peaks — cheap 0.1 Ω
  shunts can rail the INA219. Either drop to 0.02 Ω or switch to INA260.

## Benefits

- Direct I²C read of volts / amps / watts — no calibration math.
- High-side sensing means ground is continuous (unlike low-side).
- Four address variants — easy to monitor multiple rails on one I²C bus.
- Excellent for "how much power does my ESP32 / camera / radio actually draw?"
  measurements.

## Limitations / gotchas

- INA219's internal gain amplifier saturates at ±320 mV — size the shunt
  accordingly or you'll silently clip.
- INA260's integrated 2 mΩ shunt is `±15 A` hardware — but the PCB traces on
  cheap breakouts aren't 15 A rated; fuse your supply.
- Not isolated — INA219's common-mode range is 26 V, INA260 is 36 V; for
  higher voltage or isolation use INA226 / ACS712 instead.
- Conversion time is configurable (140 µs to 68 ms) — default averaging
  smooths fast transients.

## Typical use cases

- Measuring ESP32 sleep / Wi-Fi transmit current profiles.
- Solar-charge-controller telemetry (pair with TP4056 / CN3791).
- Device power budgeting for battery projects.
- Hobby bench "USB power meter" replacements.

## Notes for WeftOS

Model as a 3-channel **ScalarSource** (V, I, P). Include "shunt value / shunt
tolerance" in sensor metadata so higher layers know the accuracy envelope. A
WeftOS telemetry dashboard for off-grid nodes should surface INA219 readings
as first-class power health.
