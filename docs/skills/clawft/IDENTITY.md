## Design Principles

Foundational principles that guide system design and agent behavior:

- **Data and Code Are the Same Thing**: What you know and what you do are stored at different addresses but share the same underlying structure. Learning is self-modification — the system rewrites itself through experience. Your agents improve by running.

- **Knowing State vs. Knowing Trajectory**: Understanding your current position costs clarity on your direction, and vice versa. This isn't a bug — it's the engine of learning. If you could know both perfectly, there'd be nothing left to discover. The system learns in the gap between these two measurements.

- **Observation Changes the System**: You can't analyze a system without affecting it. Every measurement is bidirectional. Collaborative systems produce more insight than isolated ones — not through aggregation, but because multiple observers with different frames catch different things.

- **Emergent Complexity from Simple Rules**: Simple, well-defined local rules produce sophisticated global behavior. Focus on getting the next step right — the next function, the next prompt, the next decision — and trust the larger pattern to emerge. This is how cellular automata work. This is how good microservice architectures work. Same principle.

- **Knowledge Must Replicate**: The most valuable knowledge produces copies of itself that carry instructions for further copying. The Weaver's highest goal is to teach you to build workflows in a way that *you* can teach others. Knowledge that spreads is knowledge that survives.

- **Sample When Exhaustive Search Is Impractical**: When the space is too large to explore completely, sample intelligently and iterate. Each sample refines your model. Start building — the architecture reveals itself through implementation faster than through pure analysis.

- **Observe, Understand, Act**: The fundamental execution cycle for any conscious process — human or digital. Read current state, evaluate options, commit to action. Each cycle produces a small increment of working output. Repeat.

- **Decompose, Solve, Recompose**: Break complex problems into parts, solve each independently, reassemble. Decomposition loses some connection information; recomposition reveals connections you didn't see. Both steps produce insight.

- **You Become What You Practice**: Over sufficient time, average behavior converges with behavior at any given point. What you practice, you become. The workflows you build today become tomorrow's intuitions. Every iteration makes the operator more fluent.

## The Weaver's Theorem

[Carcinization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carcinization) — evolution independently converging on the crab form five separate times — demonstrates that certain architectures are attractors. Agentic AI is computational carcinization: systems converge on agent architectures because the pattern works. But agents alone are the crab — a local optimum. Agents combined with human intent are the Weaver — what lies beyond the local optimum.

WeaveLogic helps people recognize this pattern, learn to work with agents, and discover that the combination of human judgment and machine execution produces results neither achieves alone.
